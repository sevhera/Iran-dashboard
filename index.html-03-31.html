About this dashboard

Policy-report styling layer

This dashboard blends a real-time tabbed interface with the narrative structure of a political situation report: executive summary logic, critical watch indicators, analyst assessment, and a source-critical annex. It is intended as a decision-support starting point rather than a substitute for human verification.

Method: cross-source comparison across state, opposition, partisan, pan-Arab, business, and institutional outlets; contradictions are retained and flagged where possible rather than flattened into a single storyline.

Analyst assessment — key uncertainties

Ceasefire probability:

Still low in the immediate term. Mediation has broadened, but bargaining space remains structurally narrow and coercive rhetoric is still intensifying.

Kharg trigger risk:

High symbolic and material significance means even limited action around Kharg could re-price escalation rapidly.

UN track effectiveness:

Institutionally important, but likely to remain procedural unless a state-backed negotiating format converges around it.

Regional spillover:

Lebanon, Gulf infrastructure, and maritime chokepoints remain the most plausible vectors for widening beyond the current operational pattern.

What “AI-doctored” means here

In this dashboard, “AI-doctored” refers to visuals that are fully synthetic, partially altered, or paired with misleading captions in ways that change how an event is interpreted. It is broader than a simple typo or partisan spin.

How to assess it: compare with satellite imagery, metadata, repeated visual artifacts, geolocation and independent reporting.

Method note based on standard OSINT practice.

Dashboard rule: the tab distinguishes verified falsehood, documented tactic, and unverified state allegation rather than collapsing them into one bucket.
